## **Project Synopsis: Incentive-Compatible Referral Auctions on Networks**

### **Theme:** Incentive-Compatible Auctions on Networks and Referral Mechanisms

In classical auction settings, incentive-compatible and revenue-optimal mechanisms assume centralized information. However, in many real-world markets, auction information spreads through social or referral networks of strategic agents who may choose whether or not to forward information. This project studies **incentive-compatible auction mechanisms on networks**, with a focus on **referral auctions** that reward both bidding and information diffusion.

We will **implement and experimentally evaluate** several known mechanisms, including a centralized Vickrey auction, network auctions without diffusion incentives, and incentive-compatible referral auctions from the literature. We will build a simulation framework with explicit network structures and strategic diffusion behavior, and compare mechanisms using metrics such as seller revenue, social welfare, and diffusion depth.

As an extension, we will **stress-test referral auctions under relaxed assumptions**, such as **non-i.i.d. bidder valuations** and varying network topologies, to study their robustness in more realistic settings. The project is primarily implementation- and experiment-oriented, and aims to provide empirical insights into the performance of incentive-compatible network auctions beyond idealized assumptions.
